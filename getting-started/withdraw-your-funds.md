# Withdraw Your Funds

### Before You Start

Withdrawals can only be made from your **Account Balance**.

If your funds are currently in your **Trading Account**, you must first transfer them back to your **Account Balance** before starting the withdrawal.

{% hint style="info" %}
**Important:** You cannot withdraw directly from your **Trading Account** to an external wallet.
{% endhint %}

{% stepper %}
{% step %}
### Step 1: Move Funds to Account Balance

If the funds you want to withdraw are in your **Trading Account**, open the **Transfer** tab and move them to your **Account Balance** first.

Transfer supports both directions:

* **Account Balance → Trading Account**
* **Trading Account → Account Balance**

For withdrawals, use **Trading Account → Account Balance**.

{% hint style="info" %}
**Important:** Moving funds back to **Account Balance** is part of the withdrawal flow. After that, you still need to complete the withdrawal in the **Withdraw** tab.
{% endhint %}
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Step 2: Open the Withdraw Tab

Go to the **Assets** page and click the **Withdraw** tab.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Step 3: Select the Asset and Network

Choose the asset you want to withdraw and the network you want to use.

{% hint style="warning" %}
**Warning:** Always make sure the selected asset and network are correct before continuing. Withdrawing on the wrong network can result in permanent loss of funds.
{% endhint %}
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Step 4: Enter the Destination Address and Amount

Enter the wallet address you want to withdraw to, then enter the amount you want to send.

Review the details carefully before continuing.

{% hint style="info" %}
**Important:** Withdrawals can be sent to any external wallet address you used for deposit.
{% endhint %}
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Step 5: Confirm the Withdrawal

Review the withdrawal details and click **“Withdraw”** to continue.

If required, complete the confirmation steps shown in the platform.

Once confirmed, the withdrawal will be processed onchain and sent to the external wallet address you entered.
{% endstep %}
{% endstepper %}

### Fees and Confirmation

Withdrawal network fees are paid from your **Account Balance**.

If there is not enough balance available to cover the required network fee, the withdrawal may fail.

Network confirmation time depends on the selected blockchain and current network conditions.

### Minimum Withdrawal Amounts

Each asset has a minimum withdrawal amount. It helps cover practical onchain processing requirements and prevent withdrawals that are too small to be processed efficiently. If your withdrawal is below the minimum, it cannot be processed.&#x20;

Current minimums are:

* **USDT:** 5
* **YELLOW:** 1,000
* **BNB:** 0.015
* **WBTC:** 0.00015
* **ETH:** 0.005
* **WSOL:** 0.1

### Quick Reminder

To withdraw externally, follow this order:

1. **Trading Account → Account Balance**
2. **Withdraw from Account Balance to an external wallet**

<details>

<summary>Is withdrawing the same if I sign in with Google or connect a wallet?</summary>

Yes. Withdrawals follow the same process either way.

To withdraw funds, they must first be in your **Account Balance**. If your funds are in **Trading Account**, move them back using the **Transfer** tab. Then go to **Withdraw**, enter the destination address, select the asset and network, and confirm the withdrawal.

</details>

<details>

<summary>How long does a withdrawal take?</summary>

Withdrawals are typically processed within a few minutes.

</details>

<details>

<summary>What's the minimum withdrawal amount?</summary>

Minimum withdrawal amounts vary by asset. Check the form for the selected asset.

</details>

<details>

<summary>My withdrawal hasn't arrived. What do I do?</summary>

Check your transaction history. If the status is completed but funds are missing, contact [Support](../community-and-resources/support/).

</details>

<details>

<summary>Are there any withdrawal fees?</summary>

No fee for withdrawing from your Trade Account to your unified balance.

</details>
